Step 01 — Problem Definition

What problem are we solving?

Students searching for internships face a trust crisis — fake listings, unresponsive employers, and no reliable way to verify if an opportunity is legitimate. Existing platforms optimise for volume, not safety.

🚨
Fake Listings
Platforms contain scam postings, ghost employers, and misleading job descriptions that waste students' time and money.
🔍
No Verification
There's no central way to confirm if a company is real, registered, or has a track record of hiring interns successfully.
📋
Application Chaos
Students apply across multiple platforms with no unified tracker, constantly losing track of statuses and follow-up dates.
Problem Statement
"How might we help college students discover trustworthy internships while simplifying and organising the entire application process?"
Step 02 — User Research

What did students actually tell us?

We conducted a survey of 20 college students and 5 in-depth interviews with active internship seekers. The findings were clear and consistent — trust and organisation are the two dominant needs.

75%
of students surveyed said they actively fear internship scams and don't trust listings on major platforms.
70%
struggle to verify whether a company is legitimate before investing time in the application process.
65%
reported losing track of application statuses, missing follow-up opportunities, and forgetting deadlines.
60%
found recommendations on existing platforms irrelevant to their actual skills and academic background.
Research methods used
📊
Online Survey — 20 students
Google Form with 12 questions covering platforms used, fears, and feature preferences. Distributed across BCA, BBA, B.Tech batches.
🎙️
User Interviews — 5 students
15–20 minute one-on-one sessions. Participants walked through their last internship search. Key quote: "I applied but had no idea if the company was even real."
Core Insight
Students don't need more internship listings — they need confidence that the ones they see are real, and a system to manage the ones they apply for.

Step 05 — Feature Definition

Six features, six problems solved

Every feature maps directly to a pain point discovered in research. Nothing was added without a corresponding finding — that's how feature bloat is avoided.

✓
Feature 01
Verified Company Badge
Companies earn a badge by passing three checks: official website, LinkedIn profile match, and government business registration. Badge is revoked if any check fails.
↳ Solves: fake listings fear (75%)
🛡
Feature 02
Internship Trust Score
A composite 0–100 score per listing, calculated from company verification (25%), student reviews (25%), completion history (25%), and employer response rate (25%).
↳ Solves: can't verify credibility (70%)
⚠
Feature 03
Scam Reporting System
Students can report fake interviews, payment requests, misleading descriptions, or ghost listings in 3 taps. Reports trigger a review queue and can delist companies.
↳ Solves: community safety + accountability
📋
Feature 04
Application Tracker
A Kanban-style dashboard with 5 columns: Applied, Under Review, Interview, Offer, Rejected. Cards carry interview dates and send push reminders automatically.
↳ Solves: losing track of applications (65%)
⚡
Feature 05
Skill-Based Matching
Students enter skills during onboarding. The platform surfaces internships by skill compatibility score — shown as matching tags on every listing card.
↳ Solves: irrelevant recommendations (60%)
💰
Feature 06
Salary Transparency
Every listing must display exact stipend amount, benefits, work mode (remote/hybrid/office), and duration. "Competitive stipend" and other vague labels are blocked.
↳ Solves: information asymmetry

Step 12 — Outcome & Takeaways

What did this project achieve?

TrustIntern directly addresses the three core pain points discovered in research — fake listings, no credibility signal, and application chaos — with measurable improvements to student confidence and organisation.

🛡
Trust built into every step
The verified badge and trust score appear on every listing card — students never have to leave the platform to verify a company.
📊
Applications under control
The Kanban tracker with automated reminders means students never forget an interview or lose track of a status again.
💡
Transparency over vagueness
Exact stipend figures replace "competitive pay." Students know what they're applying for before they invest any time.
Key design takeaways
01
Students prioritise trust over quantity. Given the choice between 100 unverified listings and 20 verified ones, every research participant chose 20. Trust is the primary feature, not the filter.
02
Clear tracking reduces application anxiety. Usability testing confirmed that the Kanban view made students feel "more in control" — even when no status had changed. Visibility itself is valuable.
03
Transparency builds application confidence. Showing exact stipend upfront (instead of "competitive") increased perceived legitimacy. Students rated listings with transparent pay 40% higher on trust.
04
Skill-matching changes how students browse. When tags visually indicated a skill match, 3/5 participants started with those cards first — reducing search time and improving relevance perception.
